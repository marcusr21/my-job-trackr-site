Best Job Tracker Apps for UK Job Seekers (2026)

Published 10 June 2026 · 7 min read

By Marcus · Founder, My Job Trackr

Published: June 10, 2026

Most job tracking apps are built with the US market in mind — dollar pricing, US-specific job board integrations, and no native mobile app. If you're job hunting in the UK, that matters.

This is a comparison of the main job tracker apps available in 2026, written for UK job seekers. I'll cover what each tool does well, where it falls short, and which one I'd recommend depending on your situation. Fair warning: I built My Job Trackr, so take my take on it with that context in mind — but I'll be straight about the competition.

What Makes a Good Job Tracker?

Before comparing tools, it helps to agree on what actually matters. A good job tracker for UK job seekers should:

  • Track applications reliably — company, role, date, status, notes
  • Send reminders — follow-ups and interview alerts
  • Work on mobile — native iOS and Android, not just mobile web
  • Price in GBP — no surprise currency conversion on your bank statement
  • Have a real free tier — not a 7-day trial that requires a credit card

My Job Trackr — Best for UK Job Seekers

My Job Trackr was built specifically for the UK market. It's the only dedicated tracker with GBP pricing, native iOS and Android apps, deadline reminders, an offer comparison tool, and native job search — all in one place.

  • Free tier: 3 applications/month, no credit card required
  • Pro: £2.99/month on web, £3.99/month via iOS App Store and Google Play
  • Unique features: Native job search, Quick Import from job URLs, side-by-side offer comparison

Where it's still building: CV builder tools and LinkedIn integration are not yet features. But for core job tracking — applications, interviews, offers, reminders — it's the most complete option for UK users.

Huntr — Good for US Users, Less Ideal for UK

Huntr is one of the most established job tracking tools and has a clean kanban-style interface. It works well for basic application tracking. For UK job seekers the main issues are dollar pricing (no GBP option), no native mobile apps, and no deadline reminders or offer comparison.

If you want a detailed comparison: Huntr vs My Job Trackr for UK job seekers.

Teal — Feature-Rich but Complex

Teal is a comprehensive career management platform — it includes CV building, LinkedIn optimisation, job tracking, and more. It's priced in USD and is web-only (no mobile apps). For UK users who specifically need job tracking, the feature depth can feel like overkill, and the interface has a learning curve.

If you're comparing: Teal vs My Job Trackr for the UK.

Simplify — Built Around Auto-Apply

Simplify's main feature is its browser extension for auto-filling job applications. The tracking features are secondary to the application automation angle. It's primarily US-focused (most of its job board integrations are American), web-only, and priced in USD.

Full comparison: Simplify vs My Job Trackr for the UK.

Free Options: Google Sheets and Notion

If budget is the main constraint and you're applying to fewer than 5 roles at a time, a Google Sheets template is a reasonable starting point. It costs nothing, is fully customisable, and syncs across devices.

The limitations are the same as any spreadsheet: no reminders, no mobile-native experience, all manual data entry, and no offer comparison. For light use it's fine. As your search scales, it becomes a bottleneck.

Our Recommendation

For UK job seekers actively applying to multiple roles, My Job Trackr is the strongest option in 2026. It's the only dedicated tracker with GBP pricing, native mobile apps, and features that cover the full journey from application to offer.

For US job seekers, Huntr and Teal are both solid choices. For anyone on a tight budget doing a light search, a spreadsheet works fine.

Start with the free tier and upgrade when your search gets serious — that's the most sensible approach regardless of which tool you use.
