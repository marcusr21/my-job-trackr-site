Job Tracking: The Complete Guide for UK Job Seekers

Updated 20 June 2026 · 10 min read

By Marcus · Founder, My Job Trackr

Job tracking is the practice of recording and monitoring every job application you submit so nothing falls through the cracks. This guide covers everything UK job seekers need to know: what job tracking is, why it matters in 2026, what to track, how to start, and the tools worth using.

What Is Job Tracking?

Job tracking means keeping a structured record of every role you apply for during a job search. At its simplest it is a list of companies, job titles, and application dates. Done properly, it includes interview schedules, follow-up reminders, saved job descriptions, salary information, and notes from every interaction with each employer.

The goal is visibility. When you are applying to 20 or 30 roles at once, it is impossible to keep everything in your head. A job tracking system gives you a single source of truth, so you always know where you stand with each company and what needs to happen next. To understand what job tracking involves in more detail, our explainer post covers the basics clearly.

Why Job Tracking Matters in 2026

The UK job market is more competitive than it has been in years. UK unemployment is rising and now stands at 5.0%, the highest level in five years. Job vacancies have fallen to 705,000, the lowest since 2021. There are roughly 2.5 candidates competing for every open role.

In this environment, how you manage your search matters as much as what you apply to. The candidates who follow up reliably, arrive at interviews knowing exactly what they submitted and when, and respond quickly when an offer arrives are at a genuine advantage. Organisation is no longer a nice-to-have.

Job tracking also helps you spot patterns. Which sectors are responding? What role types are getting you to interview? Which application approaches are leading to better outcomes? Without data you are guessing. With a job tracking system in place, you can see what is working and double down on it.

What to Track for Every Application

The minimum information worth recording for every job you apply to:

  • Company name and job title
  • Date applied
  • Current status (applied, interviewing, offer, rejected)
  • Job URL or saved description — job postings disappear from boards once they close, and you will need the description at interview
  • Follow-up date — set this 7 to 10 days after applying
  • Interview dates and format
  • Salary and location
  • Notes — recruiter name, anything discussed, next steps

It sounds like a lot, but most of this takes less than two minutes per application if you are using a tool with auto-fill or Quick Import functionality.

How to Start Job Tracking

There are three main approaches, each with trade-offs.

Spreadsheet. Free and flexible. Works until the volume of applications and the manual effort of maintaining it becomes a drag. No reminders, no mobile sync, no interview calendar. Notion. More structured than a spreadsheet and visually appealing. Still requires you to build and maintain your own templates, and lacks purpose-built features like follow-up reminders or offer comparison. Can work well for people who already use Notion heavily.

Dedicated job tracking app. The highest-leverage option for anyone applying to more than a handful of roles. Frequently Asked Questions

What is job tracking?

Job tracking is the practice of recording and monitoring every job application you submit during a job search. A job tracking system gives you one place to log company names, job titles, application dates, interview schedules, and offer details so nothing falls through the cracks. Dedicated job tracking apps like My Job Trackr replace scattered spreadsheets with a single organised dashboard.

Why is job tracking important?

Job tracking is important because it keeps you organised when managing multiple applications at the same time. With UK unemployment at 5% in 2026 and roughly 2.5 candidates per vacancy, the job seekers who follow up reliably, prepare thoroughly for interviews, and respond quickly to offers have a real advantage over those relying on memory or a cluttered inbox. Job tracking also helps you analyse which approaches are working and where to focus your effort.

What should I track for every job application?

For every job application you should record the company name, job title, date applied, current status (applied, interviewing, offer, rejected), the job URL or description, follow-up date, interview dates and notes, and salary information. Saving the job description is especially important because postings are often removed from job boards once they close, leaving you without key details when you reach the interview stage.
